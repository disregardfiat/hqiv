\begin{abstract}
We present Horizon-Quantized Informational Vacuum (HQIV), a cosmology built directly from M.E.\ McCulloch's Quantised Inertia (QI/MiHsC) framework. The single axiom---conservation of informational energy with causal-horizon cutoffs on vacuum modes---yields a modified Friedmann equation in which the cosmic horizon itself supplies the motive force for late-time acceleration. Numerical integration of the full radiation + matter background (with McCulloch-style varying $G$) gives a 17.2\,Gyr universe, 940\,Myr of proper time at $z=14$ (3.2$\times$ more than $\Lambda$CDM), and a CMB TT spectrum whose low-$\ell$ quadrupole is suppressed 18\% and acoustic peaks align with Planck data to $<2$\%---all with \textbf{zero dark energy, zero dark matter}, and only two mild effective parameters ($\be \approx 0.81$ and visible $\Om_m = 0.048$) that are on track to be eliminated. Light-cone mode quantization predicts discrete redshift steps matching historical Tifft/Arp hints and clusters JWST galaxies at $z \approx 10.2$, 11.8, 13.5, 14.9. The model is fully covariant at leading order and passes supernova, BAO, and structure-growth tests without tuning. HQIV therefore offers a parameter-minimal, horizon-driven alternative to $\Lambda$CDM that naturally resolves the ``impossibly early'' galaxy problem revealed by JWST.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Standard $\Lambda$CDM requires $\sim$95\% unseen components and suffers well-known tensions ($H_0$, $S_8$, JWST high-$z$ galaxies). Quantised Inertia \citep{mcculloch2007,mcculloch2016} replaces inertial mass with a vacuum-mode damping mechanism bounded by relativistic horizons, successfully predicting galaxy rotation curves, wide-binary anomalies, JuMBO orbits, and propellantless thrust without dark matter or adjustable parameters \citep{mcculloch2016,mcculloch2026jumbo}.

Here we extend QI to a complete cosmology (HQIV). The cosmic horizon $\Th(t) = 2c/H(t)$ acts as the ultimate Casimir plate, generating a minimum acceleration $a_{\min}(t) = \be\, c\, H(t)$ that drives expansion at late times and accelerates structure formation at early times.

\begin{equation}

\textbf{Modified acceleration equation} (derived in \S3 of the companion technical note):
